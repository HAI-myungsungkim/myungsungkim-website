My research focuses on translating emerging technologies into meaningful real-world interventions. I work on VR-based mental health interventions, AI/chatbot-mediated support, mobile behavioral interventions, and mixed-method implementation studies, with particular interest in user acceptance, lived experience, and design for positive behavioral and clinical impact.

# 120-Word Bio
I began in human factors engineering with a minor in industrial design, and my research has evolved toward interdisciplinary work connecting cognition, behavior, affect, design, and health. I have worked across VR, AI, and mobile platforms, especially in mental health contexts, using mixed methods, user experience research, intervention design, and implementation-oriented evaluation. My recent work includes virtual reality exposure therapy, social chatbots for loneliness and social anxiety, chatbot-augmented mobile interventions, and clinician-centered studies on generative AI adoption in psychiatric care. While I remain deeply interested in mental health, I am increasingly motivated by a broader question: how can emerging technologies be designed and translated so that they improve everyday life and health in a human-centered, socially beneficial way?
